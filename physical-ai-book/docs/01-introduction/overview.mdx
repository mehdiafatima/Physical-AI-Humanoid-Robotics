## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI refers to intelligent systems that interact with the real world through their physical embodiment. Unlike traditional AI, which is confined to digital tasks, Physical AI agents perceive their environment, make autonomous decisions, and execute actions using robotic hardware. This seamless integration of perception, cognition, and physical action is what allows robots to perform complex tasks, adapt to unforeseen circumstances, and collaborate with humans in a meaningful way.

## The Rise of Humanoid Robotics

Humanoid robots, designed to mimic human form and capabilities, represent a pinnacle of Physical AI. Their development pushes the boundaries of engineering, control theory, and artificial intelligence. From advanced manufacturing to assistive roles in healthcare, humanoids are poised to revolutionize various sectors by offering versatile, adaptable, and intuitive physical interaction.

## The Simulation-First Philosophy

Developing physical robots is complex, time-consuming, and costly. Our **simulation-first** philosophy advocates for extensive testing and refinement in virtual environments before deploying to hardware.

:::info Simulation-First Benefits
-   **Accelerates Development**: Iterate rapidly without hardware constraints.
-   **Reduces Risk**: Test dangerous scenarios safely.
-   **Optimizes Performance**: Fine-tune algorithms in a controlled setting.
-   **Democratizes Access**: Develop sophisticated robots without owning expensive hardware.
:::
